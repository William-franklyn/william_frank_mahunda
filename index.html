04 AI4ALL Ignite

In Summer 2026 I was selected for AI4ALL Ignite, a national accelerator that develops emerging AI leaders through hands-on, responsible machine learning work.

With a 7-person team, I built a model predicting jet engine remaining useful life (RUL) on the NASA Turbofan (CMAPSS) dataset — the kind of predictive-maintenance problem where model errors have real safety and cost consequences. The Random Forest model reaches RMSE 18.0 / MAE 13.3 / R² 0.81 on unseen engines, and we shipped it as an interactive dashboard.

How it prepared me for product & AI roles

  • Responsible AI in practice — audited dataset bias with sub-group performance analysis and cross-validation across engine types, not just aggregate accuracy.
  • Stakeholder communication — translated model tradeoffs (precision vs. recall, downtime cost vs. safety risk) for non-technical audiences, the core skill of an AI product manager.
  • Team execution — scoped, divided, and delivered an ML project across a 7-person team on an accelerator timeline.
  • Applied ML rigor — Random Forest and Logistic Regression on multi-sensor degradation data, with disciplined evaluation methodology.
